Instructions

The application of large language model (LLM) chatbot application is growing more and more popular. With a LLM chatbot, you could talk with a artificial intelligence agent about questions or other things in real time. However, during the usage of these chatbot LLMs, there potentially could be computer system glitches that damages user experience. This survey studies how different kinds of quality damages potentially affect your experience with these LLMs. You will be asked to choose a prompt of your choice and watch response from ChatGPT, the most popular LLM chatbot. The responses will be created from a same original response by applying different quality damages. This survey is divided to two sections:

  • First part is a short training, where you will learn about different kinds of damage and how to grade them.
    1. You will first watch three short example videos. These examples show you two types of quality damage you might see in this study: startup delay (Time to First Token), and intermittent stalls.
    2. We use a 5-level grading: Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor, or Bad. After watching the short example videos, you will then watch another two reference videos. The first is an example of "Excellent" grade (no perceptible damage). The second is an example of "Bad" grade (annoying damage).
  • Second part is where you will grade the test videos. You will first go over a chat history and ask a question about the history. You will grade the video by response quality.
    1. You should grade the quality of each test video by comparing to the two reference videos you have watched.

Please be sure to watch each video in its FULL LENGTH and answer every question in this survey honestly. Otherwise your result may be NULLIFIED and you might NOT be paid.

Please take the simple survey before the test.
